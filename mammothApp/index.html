Kaitlin Dyer

Personal Statement

Hello, We Are Mammoth! My name is Kaitlin Dyer and I’m excited to apply to be your Junior Front-end Developer.

I am the perfect candidate for this position for several reasons--the least of which is that I am both a developer and a poet.

The biggest advantage to being a poet is that everything is interesting. Some might think communication and technology are disparate talents, but both (in the words of Matt Ward) are masters’ arts. Each require a deep understanding of its craft, attention to precision, and a never-ending curiosity. As a poet and developer, I have the curiosity to learn as well as the talent to create.

I have no doubt that my experience and desire to grow with your company will be a great fit!

Why Hire Kaitlin?

  • Passionate about front end web development. I have volunteered with an online academic journal, Harlot, for the past nine years because I have simply had fun developing the website. This is where I first learned how to work with HTML, CSS, jQuery, Wordpress, Smarty template engine, and PHP. I’m eager to do more with semantic code, Javascript, and other template engines.
  • Experienced telecommuter. The entire editorial board of Harlot lived all over the U.S. We used online tools to manage the publication process. Thus, I would easily adapt to the systems used at We Are Mammoth.
  • Educated and experienced in effective communication. As a published poet and recipient of a master’s degree in writing, I understand the nuance of effective communication. I also teach rhetoric and the importance of knowing one’s audience in order to persuade them.
  • Accustomed to working with diverse groups. I’m originally from Columbus, OH, now live in San Diego, CA, but spent some time living in Buenos Aires, Argentina. That has only enhanced my ability to understand others’ perspectives which allows me to work more efficiently with others. That also means that I can better understand and address clients’ concerns.
  • Fast learner. Because technology is constantly changing, we must always be willing to learn and grow with it. I look forward to We Are Mammoth’s Friday afternoon code meetups to help facilitate that growth and learning as I explore technologies individually as well.
  • Takes work seriously, but doesn’t take herself seriously. I believe having a good attitude is very important. Being positive with coworkers can create good rapport which is essential to a motivating work environment. Thus, being passionate about the work and open to others’ input is paramount to creating a successful product.
